## 1. Market Overview

The B2B payments market is undergoing its most significant structural transformation since the introduction of electronic funds transfer. Paper-based trade credit — the informal "I'll pay you in 60 days" handshake deal — is being replaced by digital, embedded, data-driven credit products. B2B BNPL sits at the intersection of three macro trends: the SMB digitization wave, the open banking revolution, and the death of the paper invoice.

**Global B2B payments market:** $125 trillion annually  
**Portion still relying on trade credit / manual terms:** ~$18 trillion  
**Digital B2B BNPL market (2025):** $15.4 billion  
**Projected digital B2B BNPL market (2028):** $50.2 billion  
**CAGR (2023–2028):** 45.7%

## 5. Supply-Side Analysis

### 5.1 Credit Infrastructure Landscape (India)

| Infrastructure | Status | Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Account Aggregator (AA) framework | Live since 2023; 50M+ consents given | Core to real-time bank data scoring |
| GSTN API | Live; real-time GST filing data available | Proxy for business revenue and health |
| CIBIL / Experian SME bureau | Live; coverage improving | Fraud and default risk signal |
| OCEN (Open Credit Enablement Network) | Pilot phase; full rollout expected 2026 | Future rails for instant credit |
| UPI transaction data | Available via AA | Cash flow proxy for micro businesses |

### 5.2 Funding Landscape

Available funding structures for B2B BNPL lending book:

| Structure | Cost of Funds | Availability | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| NBFC balance sheet | 11–14% p.a. | Requires license | Long-term scale |
| Bank co-lending | 9–10% p.a. | Partnership required | Mid-term |
| AIF / institutional credit | 10–12% p.a. | Available now | Launch phase |
| Securitization of receivables | 8.5–10% p.a. | Requires track record | Year 2+ |

**Recommendation:** Launch with AIF/institutional credit facility at ~10.5% p.a. Transition to bank co-lending agreement by Month 12 to reduce cost of funds to ~9%.

## 7. Market Trends Driving Growth

### Trend 1: Procurement Platform Consolidation
The top 5 procurement platforms in India (SAP Ariba, Zoho, Tally, Coupa, Oracle NetSuite) collectively serve 2.3M+ business users. As these platforms consolidate market share, an embedded finance partnership with 2–3 of them creates a distribution network that would take years and hundreds of millions of dollars to replicate through direct channels.

### Trend 2: GST Compliance Creating Data Richness
India's GST regime mandates quarterly (and increasingly monthly) filing for businesses above ₹1.5Cr turnover. This creates a real-time, government-verified revenue data stream that is 10x more reliable than self-reported financials — the gold standard for SMB credit scoring.

### Trend 3: Rise of the Indian D2C Segment
India has 800+ funded D2C brands and 50,000+ bootstrapped D2C businesses as of 2025 (Unicommerce). These businesses have high procurement frequency, seasonal cash flow gaps, and digital-first operations. They are the ideal PayLater Pro customer: comfortable with fintech products, high LTV, and strong repeat usage.

### Trend 4: Supplier-Led Credit Demand
Suppliers are actively looking to offer credit terms to win deals — but can't afford to carry receivables on their own balance sheet. The PayLater Pro model (supplier gets paid immediately; buyer gets terms) directly solves a supplier business problem, creating a pull-effect in our B2B distribution.
