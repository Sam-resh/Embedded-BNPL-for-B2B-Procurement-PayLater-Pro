## 1. Executive Summary

**Recommendation:** Invest $18M over 24 months to build and launch PayLater Pro, an embedded B2B BNPL product targeting the SMB and mid-market procurement segment in India, with expansion to SE Asia in Year 3.

**Projected outcomes:**
- $180M Total Payment Volume (TPV) in Year 2
- $5.04M gross revenue at 2.8% blended take rate
- Break-even at Month 19
- 5-year NPV of $42M at 12% discount rate
- IRR of 34%

**Strategic rationale:** B2B payments is the largest untapped frontier in fintech. Consumer BNPL is maturing and facing regulatory headwinds. The $7.7 trillion B2B payments market in India and SE Asia is transitioning from paper-based trade credit to digital embedded finance — and this product puts us at the center of that transition, directly inside the procurement workflows where buying decisions are made.

## 2. Market Opportunity

### 2.1 Total Addressable Market

| Market | Definition | Size (2025) | CAGR | Size (2028) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Global B2B BNPL | All B2B deferred payment volume | $15.4B | 45.7% | $50.2B |
| India B2B digital payments | Total B2B digital transaction value | $1.1T | 22% | $1.9T |
| India SMB working capital gap | Unmet formal credit demand | $530B | 18% | $873B |
| Target SAM (India mid-market) | Procurement platforms with 10–500 employees | $8.2B | 38% | $21.7B |

### 2.2 Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)

With a focused launch on SAP Ariba and Zoho Books in India:
- Year 1 SOM target: $50M TPV (0.6% of SAM)
- Year 2 SOM target: $180M TPV (2.2% of SAM)
- Year 3 SOM target (+ SE Asia): $480M TPV (5.8% of expanded SAM)

### 2.3 Why Now

Three forces are converging to make this the right moment:

1. **Regulatory tailwind:** RBI's Account Aggregator framework is live, enabling instant bank data access for credit scoring — reducing underwriting costs by ~60% vs. traditional methods.
2. **Platform readiness:** SAP Ariba, Zoho Books, and Tally have opened APIs and marketplace programs — the distribution rails are ready.
3. **SMB digitization:** Post-2020, 68% of Indian SMBs now manage procurement digitally (Redseer, 2024), up from 31% in 2019. The behavior shift has occurred; the financing layer hasn't caught up.

## 6. Risk Analysis

### 6.1 Credit Risk

**Scenario analysis on Net Credit Loss (NCL):**

| Scenario | NCL Rate | Year 2 TPV | Year 2 Credit Loss | EBITDA Impact |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Base case | 1.2% | $180M | $2.16M | — |
| Optimistic | 0.8% | $200M | $1.60M | +$0.56M |
| Stress case | 2.5% | $150M | $3.75M | ($3.75M) |
| Severe stress | 4.0% | $120M | $4.80M | Product review triggered |

**Mitigation:** Credit model trained on 3+ years of SMB GST + bank data. Human underwriting backstop for orders >₹15L. Personal guarantee required for orders >₹25L.

### 6.2 Regulatory Risk

RBI's digital lending guidelines (2022, updated 2024) require:
- Disclosure of APR at point of credit
- Cooling-off period of 3 days for loans >₹50,000
- Funds disbursed only to borrower's bank account (no pass-through to intermediaries)

**Status:** Legal review complete. Product designed to comply with all current guidelines. Regulatory monitoring cadence: monthly.

### 6.3 Competitive Risk

Resolve, Mondu, and Billie are scaling globally. None have deep India procurement platform integrations. Our moat is platform exclusivity agreements + local credit data access (GSTIN + AA framework) unavailable to foreign players.

## 7. Strategic Alternatives Considered

| Option | Pros | Cons | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| Build PayLater Pro in-house | Full control, margin retention | High capital, long timeline | **Selected** |
| Acquire a BNPL fintech | Speed to market | High premium, integration risk | Rejected — no suitable India target |
| White-label a lending partner | Low capex | Margin compression, no differentiation | Rejected |
| Partnership with NBFC | License solved | Slow decision-making, limit on product control | Considered for credit facility, not product |

## 8. Decision Criteria & Recommendation

**Go/No-Go criteria for Phase 1 launch:**
- [ ] NBFC lending partnership secured or NBFC license application filed
- [ ] At least 2 procurement platform integration agreements signed
- [ ] Credit model achieves ≥70% precision on test dataset
- [ ] RBI compliance checklist signed off by General Counsel

**Recommendation: Proceed to Phase 1**

The market window is open now. B2B BNPL in India is in the "early majority" phase of adoption. A 12-month delay would cede ground to well-funded global competitors and reduce the window for platform exclusivity agreements. The unit economics are strong (LTV:CAC = 44:1), the regulatory environment is defined, and the technology infrastructure (AA framework, GSTIN API) is ready.
